Breaking the Promise

 

When California voters approved Indian gaming in two thousand, there was an explicit promise that the casinos would be located on established reservation land only. Tribes honored the promise and built on their reservation land, respecting voters’ wishes. Prop 48 will break this promise and allow reservation shopping in urban areas. Prop 48 breaks a promise to California's Tribes that casinos would only be allowed on reservation lands. For many years, tribes have honored that promise, provided for their people and given back with millions of dollars to California charities. By opening the door to casinos in communities all across our state, we are breaking the promise and endangering the selfsufficiency of tribes that played by the rules.

Station Casinos: A Bad Actor

 

The Las Vegas business that would run this casino has a long history in multiple states of unfair labor practices. It has violated labor law over 80 times, and has been fined by the Nevada Gaming Commission and the Federal Government for violation of gaming and environmental laws.

Environment

 

Prop 48 does not protect the environment. The casino would be exempt from California Environmental Quality Act requirements, and the company that will manage the casino was already caught and punished with a major fine for violating the Clean Water Act.
