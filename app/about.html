About Proposition 48

In 2012 Governor Brown signed a first-of-its-kind gaming compact with the North Fork Rancheria – an agreement that would allow North Fork to partner with Las Vegas–based Station Casino to build a 50-table, 2000-slot machine casino in Madera County on land nearly 40 miles away from the tribe’s original reservation land.

This reservation-shopping effort clearly conflicts with the inherent promise made to California voters when they initially authorized Indian gaming in 2000 with the passage of Proposition 1A. At that time, proponents worked to assure voters that Indian gaming would remain on original Indian lands – yet the North Fork Rancheria’s attempt to jump to a location near towns and freeways seeks to directly undermine that promise, opening the door to similar such reservation attempts by other tribes throughout California.

In fact, during a lengthy battle in the California Legislature, this compact was strongly opposed by many Indian tribes who have played by the rules and limited their casino operations to their own originally restored reservation lands.

Moreover, editorial boards throughout the state have opined that this compact is bad for California and would open the floodgates for similar such reservation shopping efforts.

We urge you to join us in our efforts to reject the North Fork compact and send a message that the original promise made to voters in 2000 must be kept – not only for this first off-reservation compact, but for any similar reservation shopping that may be tried in the future.

We have officially qualified a statewide referendum on the North Fork Indian gaming compact that will be on the November 2014 statewide ballot. Please Join our coalition to stop reservation shopping and keep Vegas-style casinos out of our neighborhoods.
